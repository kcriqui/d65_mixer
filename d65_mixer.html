Mixing sums CIE 1931 2° tristimulus values of each monochromatic line; the gamut is the convex hull of the primaries. The Color Probe solves the non-negative powers that reproduce the hovered color at maximum brightness. Perceived brightness (PB) weights radiant watts by a mesopic luminous-efficiency curve — the photopic V(λ) blended toward scotopic V′(λ) as dark adaptation rises (the Purkinje shift) — plus a cube-root compression; it's a relative index, not lumens, anchored so 1 W of 555 nm at daylight adaptation ≈ 100 PB. In beam-corrected mode required watts scale with each beam's spot area at the viewing distance (Ø = diameter + distance × divergence)². Per-laser watts are the neutral-white balance; the ·max line is the limiting laser. Coherent OPSL specs are from datasheets; diode/DPSS and multimode figures are estimates. Beams assumed co-aligned. See the color & hardware research brief for how show software (Pangolin BEYOND, Lasergraph DSP) drives multi-color projectors.
